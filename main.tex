There are two distinct micro-physical processes through which freezing rain can occur, although Emily heaved her last breath knowing not a thing about the hydro-meteorology at play. 

The fact was that a hot mass of tropical air had plodded northward from the Gulf of Mexico and encountered a wall of arctic atmosphere that slid over it, forming a mantle of relatively warmer air from the ground upward to roughly one kilometer in elevation. This would've made for nothing more than a welcome respite from the frigid, lip-cracking week-and-a-half that had preceded the moment Emily's car slid off of the I-75 ramp, if it weren't for all the moisture the cold airmass had accumulated as it drifted eastward over Lake Michigan a day earlier.
